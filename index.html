Location

The wedding will be held at the medieval castle Slot Loevestein located at the intersection of the rivers Waal and Maas. Built between 1357 and 1368 on orders of the knight Dirk Loef van Horne, the castle has a rich history and served as an important strategic fortification from the time it was built until World War II.

As was the case with many Dutch fortifications, Loevestein made use of its natural surroundings (particularly the water) for its defenses and was a key part of the Hollandic Water Line, a series of water based defenses and fortifications aimed to keep the province of Holland safe from land based attacks.

From 1619 the castle was used as a state prison for political prisoners. Notable amongst its prisoners was Hugo de Groot (Hugo Grotius), a Dutch jurist and writter, who laid the foundations for international law based on natural law.

Today Loevestein is open to the public, with the castle and surrounding fortifications preserved in excellent condition. You can learn more about Loevestein on www.slotloevestein.nl.
